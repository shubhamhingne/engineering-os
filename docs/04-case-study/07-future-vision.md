# 07 — Future Vision

Where Engineering OS could go in 3–5 years — described as **product evolution**, not a feature
list. The test for anything here is whether it deepens the core thesis (artifact-first, durable,
for serious engineers), not whether it adds surface area.

## The arc

**Today — a workspace.** A single engineer takes an idea to a documented, standards-compliant
repository. The product proves that lowering the cost of engineering discipline changes what one
person ships.

**Near (1–2 yrs) — a guided practice.** The lifecycle becomes active rather than document-shaped:
agents complete stages with human checkpoints, the knowledge base lets a project reason over its
own decisions, and a mobile companion makes review possible away from the desk. The product stops
being a place you generate documents and becomes the place you *practice* engineering well.

**Mid (2–3 yrs) — a team's shared brain.** Workspaces hold teams. The standards, ADRs, and
lifecycle that one engineer used become a team's connective tissue — every new service starts from
the same opinionated baseline, and the *why* behind decisions is captured by default instead of
lost in Slack. The unit of value shifts from a repository to an organization's accumulated
engineering judgment.

**Far (3–5 yrs) — engineering memory as infrastructure.** The most valuable asset an engineering
org owns is the reasoning behind its systems, and today that reasoning evaporates. Engineering OS
could become the durable memory layer: every decision, trade-off, and lesson captured as a
first-class artifact, queryable, and feeding the next decision. Not a tool you open — a substrate
the whole practice rests on.

## What stays constant

Across every horizon, the invariants hold:

- The artifact, not the conversation, is the unit of value.
- Everything the user creates is theirs, in Git, portable.
- AI assists; the engineer decides.
- The product has opinions and earns trust by being right, not by locking you in.

If a future feature violates one of these, it belongs to a different product.

## What I am uncertain about

- Whether the wedge (individual portfolio building) expands naturally into teams, or whether the
  team product is a different company. Genuinely unknown.
- Whether "engineering memory" is a product or a feature of something larger. The far vision is a
  direction, not a commitment.

The honest version of a 5-year vision is a *direction with named uncertainties*, not a roadmap of
certainties. This is the direction; the uncertainties are real.
