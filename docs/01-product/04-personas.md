# 04 — Personas

Four personas, ordered by MVP priority. Persona 1 is the primary design target.

## 1. Priya — The Portfolio Builder (primary)

- **Role:** Senior full-stack/mobile engineer, 6–8 years, preparing for senior/staff roles.
- **Context:** Strong engineer whose GitHub doesn't show it — scattered demos, thin docs.
- **Goals:** Produce a few flagship repositories that prove product thinking and architecture,
  not just coding.
- **Frustrations:** Knows the discipline (PRDs, ADRs, CI) but applying it consistently is
  tedious; jumps to code and regrets it.
- **What success looks like:** Repos a Staff Engineer would respect, built without weeks of
  manual scaffolding.
- **Why Engineering OS:** It enforces the lifecycle she knows she should follow and removes
  the busywork.

## 2. Arjun — The Indie Hacker

- **Role:** Solo founder shipping side projects and micro-SaaS.
- **Context:** Moves fast, ships often, accumulates half-documented repos.
- **Goals:** Validate ideas quickly with a clear PRD and a clean, credible repo to share.
- **Frustrations:** Product thinking feels like overhead; documentation never gets done.
- **What success looks like:** From idea to a documented, deployable repo in an evening.
- **Why Engineering OS:** Compresses the boring-but-important parts so he can focus on the build.

## 3. Sara — The Bootcamp Graduate / Junior Leveling Up

- **Role:** 0–2 years; strong drive, gaps in architecture and process.
- **Goals:** Learn how senior engineers think about products and systems; build a standout
  portfolio.
- **Frustrations:** Doesn't know what "good" looks like; tutorials teach syntax, not judgment.
- **What success looks like:** Understands *why* each artifact exists by producing real ones.
- **Why Engineering OS:** It teaches the lifecycle by walking her through it, with examples.

## 4. The Small Team Lead (secondary, v2)

- **Role:** Tech lead at an early-stage startup with no dedicated PM or architect.
- **Goals:** Bring consistency and lightweight process to a small team's repos.
- **Frustrations:** Standards live in his head; every repo is set up differently.
- **What success looks like:** Every new service starts from the same opinionated baseline.
- **Why Engineering OS:** Shared standards and scaffolding — but team features are v2, so he
  is not an MVP target.

## Anti-persona (explicitly not for the MVP)

- **Large enterprise platform teams** needing SSO, audit, on-prem, and deep customization.
  Valuable later; out of scope now and a poor fit for an opinionated, single-user MVP.
